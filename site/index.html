What's inside

A living warband sim

Not just a defense game — a whole island that works, fights and grows around you. Every shot below is captured in-engine, on Ultra.

01In-engine · Ultra
Real-time combat

Blade, block & stamina

Soft-lock the nearest threat, then swing, crit and block on a stamina budget — or hold to charge a guaranteed-crit heavy smash. Four ork breeds, each with its own brain and bite.

  • Soft-lock targeting & combo chains
  • Charged heavy strikes & timed blocks
  • Grunts · scouts · berserkers · warp-shamans

The War Table

Spend blood-won gold

A branching upgrade tree — blade, armor, abilities, walls and defenses. Climb the Prosperity, Bulwark, Champion and Armoury lines night after night, and carry every unlock down the bloodline.

  • Four branches, dozens of nodes
  • Walls, towers, ballistae & shrines
  • Upgrades persist across heirs

Questions

Before the bell

Is Warbell really free?
Yes — completely free, no account, no ads, no microtransactions. It's an open-source passion project. If you enjoy it, a GitHub star is the only currency that helps.
What platforms are supported?
There's a signed Windows 10/11 installer today. It's built on the Bevy engine (Rust), so Linux and macOS builds are on the horizon — watch the changelog for news.
How long is a run?
A night comes every few minutes of daytime prep. A full push to breach Gnashfang Hold and end the Warlord runs a few hours — but your bloodline carries upgrades forward, so no run is ever wasted.
Do I lose everything when my hero dies?
No. When a hero falls, the next heir picks up the sword with every War Table upgrade intact. Your townsfolk are your lives — it's only over when the keep falls or the last of the line is gone.
Is there controller support?
Warbell is built for keyboard & mouse right now. Gamepad support is being explored — check the changelog for the latest.
Can I follow development?
Absolutely. The project is open on GitHub, it's also on itch.io, and every release is logged in the changelog.
